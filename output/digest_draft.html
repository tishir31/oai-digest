OpenAI Weekly Digest — April 28 – May 4, 2026

18 notable items from the week of April 28 – May 4, 2026

Earnings / Financials / Fundraising

OpenAI Misses Revenue and User Growth Targets Ahead of IPO (Apr 28) — The Wall Street Journal reported OpenAI fell short of its 1 billion weekly active user goal (reaching 900M) and missed multiple monthly revenue targets in early 2026, losing ground to Anthropic ($30B annualized revenue) and Google Gemini. AI infrastructure stocks Oracle (-7.7%), CoreWeave (-7.4%), and SoftBank (-10%) sold off sharply. CNBC (widely reported)
Also: Fortune, Bloomberg

OpenAI Finalizes $10B ‘Deployment Company’ Joint Venture with PE Consortium (May 4) — OpenAI closed a $10 billion joint venture with 19 PE investors (TPG, Brookfield, Advent, Bain Capital, SoftBank, Dragoneer). The venture embeds OpenAI engineers inside client organizations across healthcare, logistics, manufacturing, and financial services. OpenAI retains control via super-voting shares; investors receive a guaranteed 17.5% annual return over five years. Anthropic announced a rival JV with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs on the same day. Bloomberg
Also: TechCrunch

Brockman Testifies OpenAI Will Spend $50 Billion on Computing in 2026 (May 5) — OpenAI President Greg Brockman disclosed under oath that the company expects to spend $50 billion on computing power in 2026, up from $30 million in 2017. OpenAI has separately pledged approximately $600 billion in infrastructure spending through 2030. Yahoo Finance / Bloomberg

ChatGPT Go Expected to Reach 112M Subscribers as Plus Tier Declines 80% (Apr 29) — OpenAI projects its cheaper ChatGPT Go tier ($8/month) will surge 36x to 112 million subscribers this year, while ChatGPT Plus subscribers are forecast to fall 80% to ~9 million. The company expects advertising revenue shown to Go users to offset the revenue shift, projecting $59 ad revenue per user by 2030. The Information

CFO Friar Pushes to Delay IPO to 2027; Altman Wants Q4 2026 Listing (May 4) — CFO Sarah Friar is advocating a delay to 2027, citing $600 billion in accumulated infrastructure commitments and missed growth targets. CEO Sam Altman continues pushing for a Q4 2026 listing targeting a $1 trillion valuation. A 2027 timeline would allow resolution of Musk litigation and close the gap between commitments and revenue. TechTimes
Follow-up: Continues CFO-CEO tension first reported April 3 (PYMNTS). New detail: specific 2027 target and $1T valuation ask.

S&P 500 Considers Rule Change That Could Fast-Track OpenAI Index Entry (May 2) — S&P Dow Jones Indices is consulting on allowing large stocks into indexes after 6 months post-IPO (down from 12) and exempting mega-cap companies from profitability requirements. The rule change would boost passive fund demand for eventual OpenAI, Anthropic, and SpaceX IPO shares. Consultation period open until May 28. The Information

Microsoft AI Revenue Tops $9.25B Quarterly, 123% YoY Growth (Apr 30) — Microsoft disclosed total AI revenue exceeding $9.25 billion quarterly (123% YoY), including Azure server sales to OpenAI. Last quarter Microsoft said OpenAI accounted for 45% of its $627 billion revenue backlog; the company did not update that figure. The Information

Product Launches & Updates

Advanced Account Security Launches with YubiKey Partnership (May 4) — OpenAI introduced an opt-in security mode requiring passkeys or hardware security keys, disabling password/SMS recovery, shortening sessions, and automatically excluding conversations from model training. Targeted at journalists, researchers, and government officials; mandatory for Trusted Access for Cyber users from June 1. Yubico partnership offers discounted key bundles at $68/pair. OpenAI
Also: TechCrunch

Partnerships & Deals

Microsoft-OpenAI Partnership Restructured: Azure Exclusivity Ends, AGI Clause Removed (Apr 28) — OpenAI and Microsoft amended their partnership agreement effective April 27. Key changes: OpenAI can now serve products on any cloud provider (ending Azure exclusivity), the AGI clause that would have terminated Microsoft’s license is eliminated, revenue sharing is restructured (Microsoft’s payments to OpenAI end; OpenAI’s to Microsoft continue through 2030 with a cap), and Microsoft’s IP license extends through 2032. OpenAI (widely reported)
Also: CNBC, TechCrunch

OpenAI Models, Codex, and Managed Agents Launch on Amazon Bedrock (Apr 28) — Within 24 hours of the exclusivity end, AWS launched GPT-5.5, GPT-5.4, Codex coding agent, and Amazon Bedrock Managed Agents in limited preview. Open-weight GPT OSS models are available in AWS GovCloud for government customers. Usage applies toward existing AWS commitments. Part of Amazon’s up-to-$50 billion OpenAI investment announced in February. OpenAI (widely reported)
Also: TechCrunch, AWS

Pentagon Signs Classified AI Deal: OpenAI Agrees to ‘Any Lawful Operational Use’ (May 2) — The Department of Defense signed classified-network deals with seven AI companies including OpenAI, which agreed to broad “any lawful operational use” terms. The announcement followed Anthropic’s refusal to grant similar license, resulting in its exclusion from Defense Department work. The Information

Regulatory & Policy

Musk v. Altman Trial Opens in Oakland; $150B Damages Sought (Apr 28) — The trial opened April 28 before Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers with an advisory nine-person jury. Musk alleges OpenAI, Altman, and Brockman breached promises to maintain a nonprofit structure, seeking $150 billion in damages and leadership ouster. Musk testified over three days that he donated $38 million under the premise the organization would prioritize safe AI development. Liability ruling expected mid-May. NPR (widely reported)
Also: CNBC, Reuters

Musk Admits Under Oath That xAI Distilled OpenAI Models to Train Grok (Apr 30) — During cross-examination, Musk acknowledged it is “partly” true that xAI used OpenAI models to train Grok through distillation, calling it “standard practice.” The admission undercuts his claim of being harmed by OpenAI’s for-profit pivot while his own company benefited from the technology. MIT Technology Review

Brockman Discloses $30B Stake; Testifies Musk Used OpenAI Staff for Tesla (May 4) — Greg Brockman disclosed his OpenAI equity stake is worth nearly $30 billion. He also testified Musk enlisted several OpenAI employees to do months of unpaid work for Tesla Autopilot in 2017 without organizational awareness, rebutting Musk’s framing of selfless commitment to OpenAI’s mission. NBC News
Also: Rappler/Reuters, CNBC

Musk Sought Settlement Before Trial; Sent Threatening Texts to Brockman (May 4) — A court filing revealed Musk texted Brockman on April 25 to “gauge interest in settlement.” When Brockman suggested both sides drop claims, Musk replied: “By the end of this week, you and Sam will be the most hated men in America.” OpenAI argues the texts show personal motivation rather than nonprofit protection. CNN
Also: The Information

Sales Leader James Dyett Departs for Thrive Capital (May 4) — James Dyett, who led enterprise and API sales since 2023, is leaving to become Operator in Residence at Thrive Capital (an OpenAI investor run by Joshua Kushner). Technical Research / Model Releases

GPT-5.5 Instant Becomes Default ChatGPT Model (May 5) — OpenAI replaced the ChatGPT default with GPT-5.5 Instant (succeeding GPT-5.3 Instant), claiming 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes medical, legal, and financial prompts, 37.3% fewer inaccurate claims on previously flagged conversations, and ~30% shorter responses. Available to all tiers and via API. 9to5Mac

OpenAI Publishes RLHF Bug Post-Mortem: ‘Where the Goblins Came From’ (Apr 29) — OpenAI explained how GPT-5.1+ models developed a 175% increase in goblin/gremlin metaphor usage due to an RLHF reward misalignment. The “Nerdy personality” customization feature inadvertently gave outsized rewards for creature metaphors that then spread across all contexts. OpenAI
