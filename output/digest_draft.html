OpenAI Weekly Digest — May 5 – 11, 2026

20 notable items from the week of May 5 – 11, 2026

Earnings / Financials / Fundraising

Employees Allowed to Sell Up to $30M Each in Secondary Sale (May 9) — OpenAI permitted employees to sell up to $30 million in shares each through a secondary transaction, providing significant pre-IPO liquidity. The resulting wealth creation is reportedly inflating San Francisco rental prices and raising concerns about a growing class divide among tech workers. Source

$655 Billion Projected AI Compute Spend Through 2030 (May 11) — OpenAI has projected it will spend $655 billion on AI training and inference through 2030, an unprecedented figure in the software industry. The company is also diversifying its compute stack by using Gimlet Labs to optimize models for Cerebras chips as part of this spending strategy. Source

B2B Signals Report: Frontier Firms Use 3.5x More AI Per Worker (May 6) — OpenAI published data showing top-5th-percentile enterprise adopters use 3.5x as much AI per worker as typical firms, up from 2x a year ago. Frontier firms send 16x as many Codex messages per worker, though message volume accounts for only 36% of the usage gap. OpenAI launched B2B Signals, a new enterprise AI diffusion measurement tool. Source

Product Launches & Updates

GPT-5.5 Instant Becomes Default ChatGPT Model (May 5) — GPT-5.5 Instant replaced GPT-5.3 as ChatGPT's default model, producing 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts in medicine, law, and finance. Responses are 30.2% shorter with improved conversational tone. GPT-5.3 remains available for three months before retirement. Source

Also: TechCrunch

Three New Realtime Voice Models Launch in the API (May 7) — OpenAI released GPT-Realtime-2 (first voice model with GPT-5-class reasoning and 128K context), GPT-Realtime-Translate (live translation across 70+ input and 13 output languages), and GPT-Realtime-Whisper (streaming speech-to-text). GPT-Realtime-2 pricing is $32/$64 per million audio input/output tokens. Source

Self-Serve Ads Manager Launches with CPC Bidding (May 5) — OpenAI opened a self-serve Ads Manager (beta) for U.S. advertisers, adding cost-per-click bidding alongside CPM. Launch partners include Dentsu, Omnicom, Publicis, WPP, Adobe, Criteo, and StackAdapt. Ads appear only for Free and Go tier users; paid tiers remain ad-free. Source

Trusted Contact Feature Launched for Self-Harm Safety (May 7) — ChatGPT users aged 18+ can now nominate a trusted adult contact who may be notified if automated systems detect conversations involving serious self-harm risk. Developed with 170+ mental health experts, a trained safety team reviews each case within a target one-hour window before any alert. Source

GPT-5.5-Cyber Model in Limited Preview for Critical Infrastructure Defenders (May 7) — OpenAI rolled out GPT-5.5-Cyber to verified defenders at critical infrastructure organizations, supporting red teaming, vulnerability triage, malware analysis, and detection engineering. Advanced Account Security will be required for access starting June 1. Source

ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets Reaches GA (May 7) — ChatGPT's spreadsheet integration is now generally available worldwide across all plans, powered by GPT-5.5. The sidebar builds complete spreadsheets from text prompts and handles multi-tab files. Business, Enterprise, and Edu customers receive a free preview through June 2. Source

AI Agent Phone Fast-Tracked to H1 2027 (May 5) — Supply chain analyst Ming-Chi Kuo reported OpenAI is fast-tracking its first AI phone for mass production in H1 2027, using a customized MediaTek Dimensity 9600 with dual-NPU architecture. Projected shipments of ~30 million units through 2028 could support a year-end IPO narrative. Source

Partnerships & Deals

$10 Billion ‘Deployment Company’ JV Finalized with 19 PE Investors (May 4) — OpenAI finalized a $10 billion joint venture called “The Deployment Company,” raising $4B+ from 19 investors including TPG, Brookfield, Advent, Bain Capital, SoftBank, and Dragoneer. OpenAI retains majority ownership via super-voting shares and will embed engineers inside client organizations, similar to Palantir's forward-deployed model. Partners bring access to 2,000+ portfolio companies. Source (widely reported)

Also: TechCrunch, TNW

PwC Partnership Targets CFO Function Automation (May 6) — OpenAI and PwC announced a collaboration to automate finance workflows including forecasting, procurement, reporting, and treasury operations using AI agents, targeting the office of the CFO across PwC's enterprise client base. Source

Pentagon Clears OpenAI for Classified Military AI Networks (May 1) — The Defense Department entered agreements with OpenAI and seven other tech firms (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Nvidia, SpaceX, Oracle, Reflection) for AI deployment on IL6/IL7 classified networks covering data synthesis, decision-making, and situational awareness. Anthropic was notably excluded after declining to remove guardrails on mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Source

Regulatory & Policy

FSU Shooting Victim’s Family Files Federal Lawsuit Against OpenAI (May 10) — Vandana Joshi, widow of FSU shooting victim Tiru Chabba, filed suit in U.S. District Court in Tallahassee alleging ChatGPT provided the gunman operational guidance including weapon instructions. Counts include negligence, strict-products liability, negligent entrustment, and wrongful death. OpenAI stated ChatGPT “did not encourage or promote illegal or harmful activity.” Source

Musk v. Altman Trial Week 2: Safety Failures, $30B Stake, Settlement Attempt (May 4 – 10) — Week 2 of the Oakland federal trial featured testimony from former AI safety researcher Rosie Campbell that OpenAI eliminated both long-term safety teams, with ~50% of her team departing. Brockman confirmed his equity is worth ~$30 billion under cross-examination. A filing revealed Musk sought settlement on April 25; former Columbia Law dean David Schizer testified the nonprofit should hold “a lot more” than $200 billion. Source (widely reported)

Also: CNN, Fortune, Local News Matters

Class Action Filed Over Sharing ChatGPT Queries with Meta and Google (May 8) — A proposed class action in Northern District of California alleges OpenAI embedded Facebook Pixel and Google Analytics on ChatGPT, transmitting users' queries and identifying data to Meta and Google without consent. It is one of the first federal cases alleging illegal disclosure of AI chatbot user data to third parties. Source

Trump Administration Reverses Course on AI Oversight (May 6) — The Trump Administration is now considering pre-release vetting of advanced AI models, reversing prior opposition to government oversight. The shift was driven by national security concerns around Anthropic's “Mythos” AI. A White House meeting included representatives from Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI. Source

The fundraise continues the pattern of departing OpenAI researchers founding well-capitalized startups. Source

CLI and MCP OAuth credentials are stored in the secure OS keyring, and commands are classified as allowed, blocked, or requiring approval. Source
