OpenAI Weekly Digest — March 31 - April 5, 2026

10 notable items from the week of March 31 - April 5, 2026

Earnings / Financials / Fundraising

Record $122 Billion Funding Round Closes at $852 Billion Valuation (2026-03-31) — OpenAI closed the largest private funding round in history at a $852 billion post-money valuation, anchored by Amazon ($50B, of which $35B is contingent on IPO or AGI), Nvidia ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B), with participation from a16z, MGX, TPG, T. Rowe Price, and Microsoft. The round also included $3 billion raised from individual investors via JP Morgan, Morgan Stanley, and Goldman Sachs — the largest retail private placement those banks have handled. Source · TechCrunch

$2 Billion Monthly Revenue, 900 Million WAU, Enterprise at 40%+ (2026-03-31) — OpenAI disclosed $2 billion in monthly revenue with 900 million weekly active ChatGPT users and 50+ million paid subscribers. Enterprise represents over 40% of revenue and is on track to reach parity with consumer by year-end. Source

CFO Sarah Friar Reportedly at Odds with Altman Over IPO Timing (2026-04-03) — CFO Sarah Friar has raised internal concerns that the Q4 2026 IPO timeline is too aggressive given $600 billion in cloud capacity commitments and slowing revenue growth. Friar was subsequently shifted to report to Fidji Simo rather than Sam Altman directly. Source

Secondary Market Bids at ~$765B, a 10% Discount to Primary Round (2026-04-01) — OpenAI shares are trading on secondary markets at an implied ~$765 billion valuation, roughly 10% below the $852 billion primary-round price, as institutional buyers pivot toward Anthropic. Next Round Capital could not find buyers for $600 million of OpenAI shares from approximately six institutional sellers. Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs began offering shares to wealth clients without carry fees. Source

Product Launches & Updates

Codex Pay-As-You-Go Pricing for Business and Enterprise (2026-04-02) — OpenAI introduced Codex-only seats with pay-as-you-go billing at API-matched per-token rates, with no rate limits. Concurrently, the annual price of ChatGPT Business dropped to $20 per seat. Codex usage within ChatGPT Business has grown 6x since January, with over 2 million weekly users. Source

Visual Product Discovery via Expanded Agentic Commerce Protocol (2026-04-02) — OpenAI launched visual product discovery in ChatGPT through an expanded Agentic Commerce Protocol. Users can browse, compare side-by-side, and upload images to find similar items. Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair have integrated. Rolling out across all ChatGPT tiers. Source

ChatGPT Voice Mode Launches in Apple CarPlay (2026-04-02) — Hands-free CarPlay voice support now available for iOS 26.4 and later, allowing users to start and resume ChatGPT voice conversations from the in-vehicle interface. Voice-only; cannot control vehicle systems or navigation. Source

Standard Voice Mode to Retire in 30 Days (2026-04-02) — OpenAI announced a 30-day countdown to retiring Standard Voice Mode, unifying all ChatGPT users onto Advanced Voice Mode. Plus subscribers get near-unlimited access; Free users get several hours per day. Source

Partnerships & Deals

OpenAI Acquires TBPN in First Media Company Acquisition (2026-04-02) — OpenAI acquired tech talk show TBPN (Technology Business Programming Network), hosted by Jordi Hays and John Coogan, in its first media company acquisition. TBPN will retain editorial independence while reporting to Chris Lehane. The show is on track for over $30 million in 2026 revenue. Source · TechCrunch

Key Hires / Departures

Major Executive Reshuffle (2026-04-03) — CEO of Applications Fidji Simo took multi-week medical leave for POTS, with President Greg Brockman assuming product oversight. COO Brad Lightcap moved to "special projects" reporting directly to Altman. CMO Kate Rouch stepped down for breast cancer recovery. Former Slack CEO Denise Dresser assumed most of Lightcap's commercial duties. Source · TechCrunch · Bloomberg
