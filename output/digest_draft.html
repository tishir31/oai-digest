OpenAI Weekly Digest — March 22 - April 5, 2026

20 notable items covering two weeks (March 22 - April 5, 2026)

Earnings / Financials / Fundraising

Record Funding Round Grows from $120B to $122B, Closes at $852B Valuation (Mar 24 – Mar 31) — OpenAI's funding round grew from $120 billion (announced March 24) to $122 billion when it officially closed on March 31 at an $852 billion post-money valuation. Anchored by Amazon ($50B, of which $35B is contingent on IPO or AGI), Nvidia ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B), the round also included an unprecedented $3 billion from retail investors via JP Morgan, Morgan Stanley, and Goldman Sachs. Source · TechCrunch

$2B Monthly Revenue, 900M WAU, Enterprise at 40%+ (Mar 31) — OpenAI disclosed $2 billion in monthly revenue with 900 million weekly active ChatGPT users and 50+ million subscribers. Enterprise now represents over 40% of revenue and is on track to reach parity with consumer by year-end. Source

SoftBank Takes $40B Unsecured Loan to Fund OpenAI Commitment (Mar 27) — SoftBank took on a $40 billion unsecured loan with a 12-month term to cover its $30 billion commitment to the OpenAI round. The short maturity is being interpreted as a signal that lenders expect an OpenAI IPO within 2026, which would let SoftBank refinance against its appreciated stake. Source

Secondary Market Bids at ~$765B, 10% Below Primary (Apr 1) — OpenAI shares are trading on secondary markets at ~$765 billion, roughly 10% below the $852 billion primary-round price, as institutional buyers pivot toward Anthropic. Next Round Capital could not find buyers for $600 million of shares from approximately six institutional sellers. Source

CFO Friar Reportedly at Odds with Altman Over IPO Timing (Apr 3) — CFO Sarah Friar has raised internal concerns that the Q4 2026 IPO timeline is too aggressive given $600 billion in cloud capacity commitments and slowing revenue growth. Friar was subsequently shifted to report to Fidji Simo rather than Altman directly. Source

OpenAI Foundation Pledges $1B for AI Safety and Life Sciences (Mar 24) — The nonprofit arm named new leaders including co-founder Wojciech Zaremba (AI safety), Jacob Trefethen (life sciences), and Robert Kaiden (CFO), pledging to spend at least $1 billion in 2026 on AI risk mitigation and life-sciences research. Source

Product Launches & Updates

Sora Video App and API Shutting Down (Mar 24) — OpenAI announced it will shut down the Sora video app on April 26 and the API on September 24, citing unsustainable economics: the app peaked at 3.3M downloads but earned just $2.1M lifetime revenue against estimated inference costs of $15M per day. The Sora research team will pivot to world simulation for robotics. Source

ChatGPT Commerce Pivots from Instant Checkout to Visual Product Discovery (Mar 24 – Apr 2) — OpenAI abandoned Instant Checkout after poor merchant onboarding and low conversion, pivoting to an Agentic Commerce Protocol focused on visual product discovery. By April 2, Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair had integrated. Users can browse, compare side-by-side, and upload images to find similar items across all ChatGPT tiers. Source · CNBC

Codex Pay-As-You-Go Pricing + ChatGPT Business Price Cut (Apr 2) — Codex-only seats now available with pay-as-you-go billing at API-matched per-token rates, with no rate limits. Concurrently, ChatGPT Business dropped to $20/seat/month. Codex usage has grown 6x since January, with over 2 million weekly users. Source

ChatGPT Voice Mode in Apple CarPlay (Apr 2) — Hands-free CarPlay voice support now live for iOS 26.4+. Voice-only; cannot control vehicle systems or navigation. Source

Standard Voice Mode to Retire in 30 Days (Apr 2) — OpenAI will unify all users onto Advanced Voice Mode. Plus subscribers get near-unlimited access; Free users get several hours per day. Source

Adult Mode “Citron” Chatbot Shelved Indefinitely (Mar 26) — OpenAI abandoned plans for an adult-oriented chatbot after pushback from employees, investors, and advisers. The company could not reliably allow erotic content while blocking illegal material. A senior employee reportedly left over the issue. Source

Partnerships & Deals

Disney Exits Planned $1B OpenAI Investment After Sora Shutdown (Mar 24) — Disney dropped its planned $1 billion investment and the associated character-licensing deal after OpenAI announced the Sora shutdown. No money had changed hands. Source

In Advanced Talks to Buy Fusion Power from Helion Energy (Mar 23) — OpenAI is negotiating to buy electricity from Sam Altman-backed Helion Energy, targeting 5 GW by 2030 scaling to 50 GW by 2035, securing 12.5% of Helion's production. Altman stepped down from Helion's board to avoid conflicts. Source

Acquires TBPN in First Media Company Deal (Apr 2) — OpenAI acquired tech talk show TBPN, hosted by Jordi Hays and John Coogan, on track for $30M+ in 2026 revenue. TBPN retains editorial independence, reports to Chris Lehane. Source · TechCrunch

Regulatory & Policy

iyO Escalates Lawsuit Against io Hardware Venture to Trade Secret Theft (Mar 26) — Audio startup iyO filed an amended complaint against OpenAI's hardware venture io, alleging a former engineer downloaded confidential CAD files and designs and shared them with io co-founder Tang Tan, accelerating io's product development by nearly a decade. Source

Open-Source Teen Safety Policy Tools Released (Mar 25) — OpenAI released open-source teen safety policies for use with its gpt-oss-safeguard model, developed with Common Sense Media and everyone.ai. The policies address graphic violence, harmful body ideals, dangerous challenges, and romantic roleplay. Source

Key Hires / Departures

Hires Former Meta VP Dave Dugan to Lead ChatGPT Ad Business (Mar 23) — Dave Dugan, former VP of global clients at Meta, joined as VP of global ad solutions, tasked with transitioning ChatGPT ads from pilot to commercial-ready. OpenAI is requiring a $200K minimum commitment from early ad partners. Source

Hires JioStar CEO Kiran Mani as Asia-Pacific Managing Director (Mar 25) — Kiran Mani, CEO of Indian streaming platform JioStar, will lead a new APAC office in Singapore starting June, reporting to CSO Jason Kwon. India is OpenAI's second-largest market with 100M+ weekly ChatGPT users. Source

Major Executive Reshuffle (Apr 3) — CEO of Applications Fidji Simo took multi-week medical leave for POTS, with Greg Brockman assuming product oversight. COO Brad Lightcap moved to “special projects” reporting to Altman. CMO Kate Rouch stepped down for breast cancer recovery. Former Slack CEO Denise Dresser assumed most of Lightcap's commercial duties. Source · TechCrunch · Bloomberg
