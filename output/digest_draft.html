Earnings / Financials / Fundraising

Record $122 Billion Funding Round Closes at $852 Billion Valuation (2026-03-31) — OpenAI closed the largest private funding round in history at a $852 billion post-money valuation, anchored by Amazon ($50B, of which $35B is contingent on IPO or AGI), Nvidia ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B), with participation from a16z, MGX, TPG, T. Rowe Price, and Microsoft. The round also included $3 billion raised from individual investors via private placements through JP Morgan, Morgan Stanley, and Goldman Sachs — the largest retail private placement those banks have handled. Source · TechCrunch (retail angle)

$2 Billion Monthly Revenue, 900 Million Weekly Active Users, Enterprise at 40%+ (2026-03-31) — Alongside the funding announcement, OpenAI disclosed it is generating $2 billion in monthly revenue with 900 million weekly active ChatGPT users and 50+ million paid subscribers. Enterprise now represents over 40% of revenue and is on track to reach parity with consumer by year-end. CFO Sarah Friar said the company is growing revenue four times faster than Alphabet and Meta at comparable stages. Source

CFO Sarah Friar Reportedly at Odds with Altman Over IPO Timing (2026-04-03) — CFO Sarah Friar has raised internal concerns that the Q4 2026 IPO timeline is too aggressive given $600 billion in cloud capacity commitments and slowing revenue growth. Friar was subsequently shifted to report to Fidji Simo rather than Sam Altman directly. Source

Secondary Market Demand Sinks to ~$765B Valuation, a 10% Discount to Primary (2026-04-01) — OpenAI shares are trading on secondary markets at an implied ~$765 billion valuation — roughly a 10% discount to the $852 billion price set in the primary round — as institutional buyers pivot toward Anthropic. Secondary marketplace Next Round Capital reported it could not find buyers for $600 million of OpenAI shares from approximately six institutional sellers, while Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs began offering OpenAI shares to wealth clients without carry fees. Product Launches & Updates

Codex Pay-As-You-Go Pricing Launched alongside ChatGPT Business Price Cut (2026-04-02) — OpenAI introduced Codex-only seats with pay-as-you-go billing — no rate limits, billed per token at API-matched rates — for ChatGPT Business and Enterprise customers. Concurrently, the annual price of ChatGPT Business dropped to $20 per seat per month. Codex usage within ChatGPT Business has grown 6x since January, with over 2 million weekly Codex users overall. Source

Visual Product Discovery in ChatGPT via Expanded Agentic Commerce Protocol (2026-04-02) — OpenAI launched visual product discovery in ChatGPT through an expanded Agentic Commerce Protocol, allowing users to browse, compare side-by-side, and upload images to find similar items. Initial retail integrations include Target, Sephora, Nordstrom, Best Buy, The Home Depot, and Wayfair. The feature is rolling out across all ChatGPT tiers. Source

ChatGPT Voice Mode Launches in Apple CarPlay (2026-04-02) — OpenAI added hands-free CarPlay voice chat support, allowing users on iOS 26.4 and later to start and resume ChatGPT voice conversations directly from the in-vehicle interface. The feature is voice-only and cannot control vehicle systems or access maps. Source

OpenAI Announces 30-Day Retirement of Standard Voice Mode (2026-04-02) — OpenAI said it will retire Standard Voice Mode within 30 days, unifying all ChatGPT users onto Advanced Voice Mode. Plus subscribers will receive near-unlimited access to the new mode; Free users will receive several hours per day. Source

Partnerships & Deals

OpenAI Acquires TBPN in First Media Company Acquisition (2026-04-02) — OpenAI announced its first acquisition of a media company, buying tech talk show TBPN (Technology Business Programming Network), hosted by Jordi Hays and John Coogan. TBPN will retain editorial independence while reporting to OpenAI's chief political operative, Chris Lehane. The show is on track for over $30 million in 2026 revenue per the Wall Street Journal. Source · TechCrunch

Regulatory & Policy

OpenAI Publishes Detailed Explanation of Its Model Spec Framework (2026-03-31) — OpenAI published a detailed explanation of its Model Spec, the public framework defining how OpenAI's models adjudicate between obligations to users, developers, and OpenAI itself. The post details an updated authority hierarchy (Root > System > Developer > User > Guideline) and new principles for agentic actions and teen-user safety. Source

Federal Judge Affirms Order Requiring 20 Million ChatGPT Logs in Copyright Case (2026-04-01) — US District Judge Sidney Stein affirmed a magistrate judge's order requiring OpenAI to produce 20 million anonymized ChatGPT conversation logs in the consolidated AI copyright litigation brought by The New York Times, Chicago Tribune, and other publishers. The court rejected OpenAI's privacy objections, finding the logs relevant to plaintiffs' fair-use analysis even where they do not directly reproduce copyrighted works. Source

Key Hires / Departures

Major Executive Reshuffle: Simo on Medical Leave, Lightcap to Special Projects, Rouch Steps Down, Dresser Elevated (2026-04-03) — OpenAI announced a four-part leadership reshuffle on April 3. CEO of Applications Fidji Simo took multi-week medical leave for postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome (POTS), with President Greg Brockman assuming product oversight including the desktop super app project. COO Brad Lightcap moved to a new "special projects" role focused on complex deals and investments, reporting directly to Sam Altman. CMO Kate Rouch stepped down to focus on late-stage breast cancer recovery. Former Slack CEO Denise Dresser, OpenAI's CRO, assumed most of Lightcap's commercial duties. Source · TechCrunch · Bloomberg · Axios

Technical Research / Model Releases

OpenAI Completes Phased Retirement of GPT-4o (2026-04-03) — OpenAI completed the full retirement of GPT-4o across all ChatGPT plans, closing out a phased sunset that began February 13, 2026. The move consolidates consumer tiers on GPT-5.4 ahead of the forthcoming GPT-5.5 release. GPT-4o remains available in the API. Source
